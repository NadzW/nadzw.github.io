  <p>This project was a complete UI/UX redesign of the GT Mode menu from <i>Gran Turismo 5</i>. The original menu felt overwhelming, cluttered, and unintuitive for new 
  players. My goal was to modernize the interface by introducing a clean, consistent, and genre-appropriate design - one that better aligns with modern UX principles and sim 
  racing aesthetics.</p>

  <h2>My Process</h2>  
  <h3>Identifying the Issues</h3>
  <p>The original GT Mode interface:<br>
  - Lacked structure and clarity<br>
  - Was unintuitive for new users<br>
  - Had poor visual hierarchy and navigational flow<br></p>
  <a href="/assets/images/blogs/gt5/gt5-originalui.jpeg" data-lightbox="gt5-originalui">
  <img src="/assets/images/blogs/gt5/gt5-originalui.jpeg"></img>
  </a>
  <h3>Research</h3>
  <p>I conducted a comparative analysis of other sim racing games like:<br>
  - <i>Forza Motorsport 5</i> - clean grid-based tile menu with clear visual cues<br>
  - <i>Project CARS 2</i> - sharp, skewed elements evoking speed and competition</p>
  <p>These examples helped shape my approach in both layout and visual theming.</p>
  <a href="/assets/images/blogs/gt5/gt5-research.jpeg" data-lightbox="gt5-research">
  <image src="/assets/images/blogs/gt5/gt5-research.jpeg" type="jpg"></image>
  </a>
  <h3>Design Decisions</h3>
  <p>- <i>Grid-based Tile System</i>: Inspired by Forza, I introduced a simplified tile layout that makes navigation faster and more intuitive.<br>
  - <i>Categorized Menu Layout</i>: I split GT Mode into three core categories to reduce screen clutter and make options easier to locate.<br>
  - <i>Racing-Themed Visuals</i>: Leveraged sharp edges, bold fonts, and speed-inspired imagery to reflect the sim racing genre.<br>
  - <i>Cover Image Buttons</i>: Each menu tile features themed background art relevant to its function, improving visual context.</p>
  <h3>Outcome</h3>
  <p>The final prototype was built and tested in <i>Figma</i>, with: <br>
  - Improved clarity and structure<br>
  - More intuitive navigation<br>
  - Stronger thematic consistency with the racing genre<br>
  <br>
  I truly believe I’ve reached my goal of improving the game’s interface and implemented the design thoroughly.</p>

  <h3>Self-Reflection</h3>
  <h4>What Went Well:</h4>
  <p>- Extensive research and planning contributed to a well-organized, genre-appropriate redesign.<br>
  - Clear categorization and visual hierarchy made the menu easier to use and more immersive.</p>
  <h4>What I’d Improve:</h4>
  <p>- Readability of button text: The stroked text effect clashed with certain background images, making some tiles hard to read from a distance.<br>
  In the future, I would explore alternate text treatments or add dynamic contrast overlays for better legibility.
  </p>
